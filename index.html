More choice, better value

Changing the amount of extras services, claim-back percentage and annual limits will change your premium - the total cost of your cover. If you're planning on using your Extras services regularly, you may wish to choose the higher limits and higher percentage back to maximise your cover.

  • Choosing 6 services means you'll have a broader range of services you're covered for.
  • Choosing 60% back means you'll get more money back when you make a claim at any recognised provider (up to annual limits).
  • Choosing higher limits means you'll have more money to make claims for a particular service (up to annual limits).

What is Extras cover? expand_more expand_less

Not everything that keeps you healthy is covered by Medicare. That's why Extras cover can be a big help. FLEXtras gives you more choice and flexibility around how you use your cover, and what you are covered for. Some of the common Extras services include:

  • Dental check-ups and cleans
  • Physiotherapy, chiropractic, occupational therapy, and podiatry
  • Optical products, such as prescription glasses and contact lenses

    • Providers of Extras services need to be recognised by Bupa so we can pay towards the cost of your treatment.

    What are the percentage back amounts? expand_more expand_less

    With Bupa FLEXtras, you can select the percentage amount you can claim back at any recognised provider. You can choose from either 50% back or 60% back. For example, if you select 50% back and you visit your physio, where you spend $100, you'll get $50 back.

    You'll only need to pay the difference between the total amount charged by your provider and the benefit Bupa pays. Keep in mind that you'll only be able to claim up to your annual limit.

    What are annual claim limits? expand_more expand_less

    An annual limit is the maximum amount of money you can claim for a particular service within a calendar year (1 January â€“ 31 December).

    We recommend choosing standard limits if you plan on using your Extras every now and then. For example, you plan on visiting the physio three to four times a year.

    We recommend selecting higher limits if you plan on using your Extras regularly. For example, you plan on visiting the physio for regular treatment every month.

    Can I update my FLEXtras policy if my life circumstances change? expand_more expand_less
    Yes, absolutely. You can change your FLEXtras selections at any time through myBupa, provided you haven't claimed for that service in the current calendar year. If you've claimed for the service you want to change, you'll need to wait until your annual limits reset on 1 January.
    Will I have to re-serve my waiting periods if I switch to a FLEXtras policy? expand_more expand_less
    If you're an existing health insurance customer and have served your waiting periods, you'll only need to serve waiting periods for services that are not included on your current cover. If you don't have health insurance then standard waiting periods apply.
